The Product

In The Air, Tonight was a month-long reactive architecture installation that transformed the LED facade of the Ryerson School of Image Arts and Ryerson Image Centre into a glowing beacon, drawing attention to the growing problem of homelessness.

Blue wave-like animations on the building visualized the realtime wind speeds in Toronto; cold weather is poses danger for those experiencing homeless in the winter months of Toronto. Red pulses were triggered by tweets containing the hashtag #homelessness to visualize the online conversation about this issue

By visiting intheairtonight.org people could read and retweet messages from the (@itatonight) Twitter feed or compose their own message, and participants can watch the building change colours via webcam. Participants could also donate to various organizations that provide support and advocacy for people experiencing homelessness such as Toronto's Out of The Cold and Raising the Roof. Every message with the hashtag #homelessness amplifies the online conversation.
