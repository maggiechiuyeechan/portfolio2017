The Challenge

At BlueDot, our team aims to create analyses creating timely insights to mitigate the spread of infectious disease. These analyses at times require knowledge translation to various parties including the general public, published in journals and news outlets and public health organizations such as US CDC (United States Centers for Disease Control and Prevention), ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations) and WHO (World Health Organization).

The Product

The following maps were produced by collaborating with a team of health and data scientists for the purposes of public health readiness, awareness and policy-making.

On February 1st, 2016, the World Health Organization declared that the association of Zika infection with clusters of microcephaly and other other neurological disorders constituted a Public Health Emergency of International Concern.

On January 14th, 2016, this figure was produced to accompany the article Anticipating the international spread of Zika virus from Brazil, published in the Lancet Infectious Diseases journal.

“In May, 2015, locally acquired cases of Zika virus—an arbovirus found in Africa and Asia-Pacific and transmitted via Aedes mosquitoes—were confirmed in Brazil. The presence of Aedes mosquitoes across Latin America, coupled with suitable climatic conditions, have triggered a Zika virus epidemic in Brazil, currently estimated at 440 000–1 300 000 cases. Viraemic travellers have now introduced Zika virus into at least 13 additional countries, where susceptible Aedes mosquitoes have become infected and perpetuated local transmission cycles.”

September 1st, 2016, another series of maps was published for the article “Potential for Zika virus introduction and transmission in resource-limited countries in Africa and the Asia-Pacific region: a modelling study” in the Lancet Infectious Diseases journal.

“As the epidemic of Zika virus expands in the Americas, countries across Africa and the Asia-Pacific region are becoming increasingly susceptible to the importation and possible local spread of the virus. To support public health readiness, we aim to identify regions and times where the potential health, economic, and social effects from Zika virus are greatest, focusing on resource-limited countries in Africa and the Asia-Pacific region.

Many countries across Africa and the Asia-Pacific region are vulnerable to Zika virus. Strategic use of available health and human resources is essential to prevent or mitigate the health, economic, and social consequences of Zika virus, especially in resource-limited countries.”

I converted the above series of static maps into a prototyped interactive web-based data visualization using HTML/CSS and D3.js.
