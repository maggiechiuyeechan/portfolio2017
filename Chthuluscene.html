The Product

The Athropocene period is our current geological age viewed as the period during which human activity has been the dominant influence on climate and the environment.

“Chthuluscene” is an interactive, co-creative, participatory, audio-visual installation exhibited at Nuit Blanche 2015 on October 2th, 2015. It provided an artistic experience to more than a 12,000 people over the course of a 12 hour period.

The installation used storytelling, and gamification techniques to engage participants in a experimental experience for the one night art event in hopes of encouraging behavourial changes and self-reflection. The image of a planet resembling earth was projected 60ft. across the space, representing a modelled simulation, a metaphor and a narrative of the processes and affects of humankind’s creation — our ecological era - the Anthropocene.

The installation invited visitors to use their mobile phones to “pair up” with a random partner in the crowd through a series of physical gestures and movements. This performative pairing produced a co-created entity on the slowly revolving planet. The digitally facilitated social interaction created a collectively shared experience. Ambiently, DJ Egyptrixx provided an atmospheric, live soundtrack to accompany the installation.

Exhibited at Nuit Blanche 2015

THE APPROACH

Throughout the process of creating the project, we spent a large part of our time conceptualizing the experience, focused on brainstorming collectively as well as employing other creative ideation techniques such as undergoing an “exquisite corpse”-styled brain writing exercise. Simultaneously, we did literature reviews to familiarize ourselves with the current academic discourse around the topic of the “anthropocene”. We drew inspiration and created collaborative moodboards, looking at both generative art and eco-art projects. As the concept began to solidify and we had ideas for a general sense of where to take the project, we began concept sketching, prototyping, interviewing artists who was familiar with creating works for a large gathering, creating process-flow diagrams of the experience and after several mid-fidelity prototypes we moved onto the final production of the experience.
