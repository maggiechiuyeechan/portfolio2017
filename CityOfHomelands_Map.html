City of Homelands Maps

Purpose

Final year student project

Year

2013

Type of project

Data Visualization

Main Tools

Mapbox/Tilemill, QGIS, LibreOffice, Adobe Illustrator

“The fragmentation of urban space and the social body create a need for maps that would enable a situational representation on the part of the individual subject to that vaster and properly unrepresentable totality which is the ensemble of society’s structures as a whole.” - McDonough, 256

The Project

Two large maps were created to supplement the video content in the multimedia installation. They visualize the distribution of language across Toronto’s geography. By combining census data and with personally collected data, the maps illustrate the relationship between the ‘immobile’ immigrant and her place as it is shaped by the social construction of her community and its inhabitation of spatiotemporal linguistic worlds.

“All social processes have substantial spatial components, which in one way or another are materialized, and thus rendered empirically observable in physical spaces.” - Manual Castells
